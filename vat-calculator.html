Everything You Need to Know About VAT in Kenya

What Is VAT?

VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax placed on a product whenever value is added at each stage of the supply chain. In Kenya, it is governed by the VAT Act 2013.

What Are the Current VAT Rates?

As of 2025, the standard VAT rate in Kenya is 16%. Some goods and services may be zero-rated or exempt based on the tax classification.

Who Should Charge VAT?

Businesses registered for VAT in Kenya (with annual turnover of Ksh 5 million and above) are required to charge VAT on taxable goods and services.

How to Register for VAT

Visit the KRA iTax portal, log in using your PIN, and apply for VAT registration. Once approved, you're expected to file monthly VAT returns and remit collections to KRA.
